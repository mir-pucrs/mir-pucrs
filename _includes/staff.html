Rodrigo Barros

Professor in Computer Science

Dr. Rodrigo C. Barros is a specialist in machine learning and data mining research. His research interests also include biologically-inspired computational intelligence algorithms, primarily focusing on evolutionary computation solutions to data mining and machine learning problems. In 2012, Dr. Barros spent a year as a visiting research assistant at the School of Computing, University of Kent, UK, working on the development of novel machine learning algorithms. Dr. Barros received the best doctoral thesis in Computer Science from the Brazilian Computer Society, and also the CAPES Award of Best Thesis from the Brazilian research agency CAPES, both due to his outstanding doctoral thesis on the automatic design of machine learning algorithms. He received his Ph.D. degree in Computational Mathematics and Computer Science from ICMC-USP, Brazil, in 2013, and his M.Sc. degree in 2009 from FACIN-PUCRS.
