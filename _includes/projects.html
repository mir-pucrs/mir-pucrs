Project Name

Automatic Reasoning about Contractual Clauses

Norms play an important role in society defining expected behaviors from society members in terms of deontic statements of obligations, permissions, and prohibitions. Norms are also used to define what is expected from parties once formal contracts are signed. Here, norms may conflict between each other when they have opposite deontic meanings, for example, when a contract prohibits something that is explicitly permitted elsewhere in the contract. Such conflicts can create contractual inconsistencies that often void them. In this project, we propose an approach to extract norms and reason about them by identifying deontic conflicts. By reasoning about norms, we aim to give the first step to contract automation.

A demo of the working conflict detection system is available at the LSA Serve

Demand Forecasting

Demand forecast allows companies to allocate resources on the right products and their respective parts at the right time. Accurate predictions often rely on the availability of reliable past demand data and any other data that may indicate tendencies of consumer behavior. As part of the demand forecasting project, we studied the problem of demand forecasting exclusively from past demand structured data. We also study separately consumer sentiment regarding products using unstructured data (text) from social media (Twitter). In this project we brought together the contributions from our prior research and design an agent architecture that enhances traditional demand forecasting with data from such social media. Our research hypothesis is that by tracking consumer sentiment alongside consumer demand, we would be able to improve demand prediction. Our empirical experiments showed an inconclusive result of the enhancement of the predictions, given a lack of data from both demand forecasting and social media. The minimal overlap between the demand data and social media sentiment also contributed for this.

Project Name

Programa de Formação e Captação em Sistemas de Informação.

Hewlett Packard Enterprise carries out joint activities with Universities and Acknowledged Research Centers. PUCRS has shared its knowledge and experience with an emphasis on Computer Science Research through Technology Centers. Human Resource training is paramount for companies like Hewlett Packard Enterprise for it to continue developing quality products using advanced technology. The main goal of this project is to train graduate level personnel at both MSc and PhD levels in the existing research lines at the Graduate Course in Computer Science of the School of Computer Science as well as the Graduate Course in Business of the Business School in themes that align with the interests of Hewlett Packard Enterprise. Expected results include: MSc dissertations and PhD theses; and scientific papers related to that research.
